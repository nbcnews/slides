The Story of UX

The Interactive Era

1997

  • Original Roger Black design
  • As impractical as it was innovative
  • Saddled designers with daily production

1999

  • Design evolved slowly
  • Limited by bandwidth, screen size
  • Newsmenu was 'innovative'

2000

  • Built rep for visual/interactive storytelling
  • Production took a day or two

2001

  • Features became increasingly ambitious
  • Experimented with combining media elements

2002

  • Staff ability advanced with tech
  • Vanity projects won awards, yet took weeks

2005

  • Team of 3 eventually produced interactive TV
  • Experimented with auto-advancing, tracking

2007

  • Site design suffered

Troubled times

  • Interactive storytelling proved difficult to scale
  • Org structure got in the way of collaboration
    • Developers in Technology
    • Designers, Interactive Producers & Concepts
      separate groups within Editorial
    • Nobody dedicated to site design
  • Economic downturn brought scrutiny on ROI
  • Strained relationship between Tech & Editorial
  • Design staff's skills atrophied

Collision Course

  • Design, IP and Concepts consolidated
  • Focus shifted from interactives to site design

2006

  • Creative ventured into sub-site dev
  • Still invested in expensive one-offs

2008

  • Out-sourced design to editors
  • Creative vs Tech tension deepened

2008

  • Yet productivity increased ...

2008

  • Creative team grew in number and talent

2008

  • Election presentation was envy of industry
  • Data distributed everywhere
  • Big homepage video
  • True multimedia experience

Faster, better ... and still falling behind

  • Pace of industry change accelerated
  • Technology and increasingly dev-oriented Creatives
    struggled to collaborate internally
  • iPhone launched, social media ramped up
  • Business needs changed, requiring more scale
  • Creative felt limited by an aging tech platform

Creative Development

  • Creative combined with parts of Technology
  • No longer editorially focused with ad team
  • Set out to finish site redesign

2010

{story pages}

  • Built on a new tech platform, SkyPad
  • Creatives began developing alongside Tech
  • Strategy: Depth, combining commodity content with multimeda assets
  • Ad tiers worked around pageview problem
  • Increased RPM per page
  • Increased video streams, later time spent
  • Kept iterating
  • Big research focus: usability, A/B testing
  • Found analytics foundation deelpy flawed
  • Editorial strategy shifted 1 year later

2010

{blog pages}

  • Meanwhile, small blog investment on Newsvine
  • Nimble approach, virtually no stakeholders
  • Supposted to be temporary format

2010

{mobile pages}

  • Also, small Creative investment in mobile web
  • Preceded 'responsive design' approach

2011

  • Creative team not organized to diversify brand UX
  • TODAY team frustrated at first
  • Led to a shift toward dedicated brand creative teams

2011

  • Creative / Tech partnership grew
  • Team ventured into editorial tools
  • Beleived custom tools needed to power custom experiences

2011

  • Creative Lab team built Breaking News
  • Small team chose outside tech platform
  • Spun out of less-centralized Creative

2012

  • Editorial shifted to franchise strategy
  • Small team built cross-device demo
  • Iterations lead to usability, A/B tests, pilot
  • SkyPad technology with content from Newsvine
  • Engagment increased, particularly on devices
  • Experiment in 'continuous consumption'
  • Unsure of content strategy with ownership changed
  • Invested in FLOW editorial tool over new features
  • Blue site creative team focused on elections, slow to take ownership of experience

2012-3

  • Prototypes took continuous consumption a step further
  • Built on top of Streams dev, Flow tool
  • Example of brand creative team owning their product
  • Rollout continues
  • Likely model for Blue site interim design
  • Measurments most encouraging I've seen

Leasons learned

  • App-like experiences are the future of UX
  • Building editorial tools distracts us from this
  • Dedicated brand teams must lead product design
  • Keeping up with change is a full-time job

Living on the edge

  • Themes from the past tied to the future:
    • What should we focus on?
    • How do we move more quickly?
    • Are we organized to succeed?

Single Page Applications

{example: Quartz?}

  • It's an app world. Examples: Quartz, USAToday
  • Study 'play-listing' and context
  • Separate front-end from back-end through APIs
  • Requires robust NewsAPI
  • Design for devices first

Build. Measure. Learn.

  • Iterate on a framework that makes it easy to experiement.
  • Prototype everything. Use real content & ads.
  • Always be A/B testing. Make data-driven decisions.
